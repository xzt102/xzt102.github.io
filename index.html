About

I am a member of Microsystems Design Lab (MDL) at Penn State, where I work with my advisor Dr. Mahmut T. Kandemir. I am also collaborating with Dr. Chita R. Das and some brilliant graduate students in the High Performance Computing Lab.

My research interests lie in the area of both complier and computer architecture. More specifically, I am interested in performance and energy-efficient GPU platform design by exploring compiler and runtime system. I am currently working on modeling, application and architecture of NVIDIA new microarchitecture such as Kepler/Maxwell.

Research Experience

  • Modeling, Architecture, and Applications for Emerging GPU Architectures. 2014 - Present.
  • Design space exploration on Kepler and Maxwell micro-architecture using simulation based approach. 2014 - Present.
  • Understanding Performance Degradation within Co-running OpenCL Applications on Integrated Heterogeneous Processors. Discussed fairness and proposed several approaches, such as kernel combination and CPU core reserving, to alleviate degradation. 2014.
  • Built a two-layer video codec benchmark suite from multimedia domain (X264, XVID, VP8). Modified X264 video codec to a fine-grain pipeline task level parallel version. 2011 - 2013.
  • Analyzed program phase characteristics while running rodinia benchmark suite on GPGPU-SIM. 2010 - 2011.
